# Fees

### Trading Fees (Platform Fees)

Trading tools and platforms usually charge a small fee when you use them to execute trades.

On **Trench**, this fee is **up to 1% of the transaction amount**.

#### Example

If you buy a token worth $100:

* Up to $1 is charged as a trading fee

**Note:** As you move up tiers, higher cashback lowers your effective fees — making trading on Trench increasingly efficient.

***

### Network Fees (Gas)

These are fees paid to the blockchain to process your transaction.

* Required for every trade
* Varies depending on network activity
* Not controlled by Trench

***

### Priority Fees

Priority fees help your transaction get processed faster.

* Higher fee → faster execution
* Useful during launches or high activity

***

### Bribes/Tips

Bribes, also known as **tips**, are optional fees that improve your transaction’s chance of being included faster.

* Common in competitive trading environments
* Helps your transaction get prioritized over others

***

### Launchpad Tax

Some tokens include a **built-in tax set by the launchpad or token creator**.

* This tax is applied automatically during buy/sell transactions
* It varies depending on the platform and token mechanics
* Not controlled by Trench

#### Example

* Bags launchpad (Solana) → \~2% tax
* Bonk.fun (Solana) → \~0.3% to 1.25% tax depending on conditions

This tax may differ based on:

* Pre-bonding vs post-bonding stage
* Launchpad rules and token design

***

### Why Fees Matter

Fees impact:

* Your entry price
* Your exit profitability
* Your execution speed

Understanding fees helps you **balance cost vs speed**.

***

### Summary

* Trading fee → paid to Trench
* Network fee → paid to blockchain
* Priority & bribes → paid to validators for faster execution
* Launchpad tax → set by token creator/launchpad (not controlled by Trench)
